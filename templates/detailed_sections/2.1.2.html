Detailed Stock Level Analysis

Analysis Aspects:

• Trends in Stock Levels: Analyze changes in the current stock levels over time for different products.

• Optimal Stock Level Analysis: Compare the current stock levels with the optimal stock quantities to identify overstocked or understocked situations.

• Stock Depletion Rates: Determine how quickly stock levels are being depleted for each product.

• Potential Inventory Issues: Identify products with consistently high or low stock levels compared to their optimal values.

Key Findings:

The analysis of the stock data for various products throughout January 2020 reveals important trends and implications for inventory management. Starting with the Face Serum, it has maintained a relatively high current stock level, peaking at 395 units and gradually decreasing to 331 units by the end of the month. Despite a consistent demand indicated by an optimal stock quantity of 34, the current stock far exceeds this, suggesting a potential overstock situation that could tie up capital.

The Gaming Mouse exhibits a concerning trend as well, starting with 237 units and declining to 79 units. This substantial drop indicates a high turnover rate, yet the optimal stock of 78 is still not achieved at the end of the month, signaling the need for replenishment to meet ongoing demand.

For the Power Bank, there was a significant decrease in stock from 66 to 30 units early in January, which raises concerns about potential stockouts. However, an anomaly occurs on January 24th, when the stock dramatically increases to 500, suggesting an influx of inventory that could lead to overstocking if demand does not rise correspondingly.

The Scented Candles show a steady decrease from 500 to 317 units, despite an optimal quantity of 230. This trend indicates that while demand exists, there may be room to optimize stock levels more effectively to avoid wastage or markdowns.

The Wireless Earbuds reflect a gradual decline from 224 to 140 units, again indicating a steady demand but not aligning with the optimal quantity of 28. Similar trends are observed with the Yoga Mat and Hydro Bottle, both demonstrating significant fluctuations in stock levels, with the Hydro Bottle showing a particularly high optimal stock requirement of 229.

Current stock levels for Cotton Sheets and Comfort Pillows also reflect a trend towards depletion, although their optimal quantities are comparatively lower. The Yoga Mat experienced a surge to 500 units on January 20, raising questions about inventory management practices and demand forecasting.

In summary, while some products show strong sales potential, others are at risk of overstocking or stockouts. Moving forward, the following recommendations can be made:

  • Conduct Demand Forecasting: Regularly analyze sales data to anticipate future demand more accurately, especially for fast-moving items like Gaming Mice and Wireless Earbuds.
  • Implement Inventory Management Software: Utilize technology to track inventory levels in real-time, helping to maintain optimal stock levels and avoid overstock or stockouts.
  • Adjust Reorder Points: Reassess reorder points based on recent sales trends and fluctuations to ensure timely replenishment of popular products.
  • Promote Slow-Moving Items: Develop marketing strategies to boost sales of items with declining stock levels, especially those nearing their optimal quantity.
  • Inventory Audits: Regular audits should be conducted to identify discrepancies between stock levels and optimal quantities, aiding in more effective decision-making.

By implementing these strategies, the company can enhance its inventory management practices, ensuring a balance between supply and demand while optimizing capital utilization.
